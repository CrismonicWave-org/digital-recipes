# Cooking Temperature Guide

**🔥 Safe internal temperatures and cooking heat levels for perfect results every time.**

This guide ensures food safety and optimal cooking outcomes. Always use a food thermometer for accuracy.

---

## Safe Internal Temperatures (USDA Guidelines)

### Poultry

| Protein | Minimum Safe Temperature | Notes |
|---------|-------------------------|--------|
| **Chicken (whole)** | 165°F (74°C) | Thickest part of breast and thigh |
| **Chicken (pieces)** | 165°F (74°C) | All parts: breast, thigh, wing, drumstick |
| **Turkey (whole)** | 165°F (74°C) | Thickest part of breast and innermost thigh |
| **Turkey (ground)** | 165°F (74°C) | Cook thoroughly, no pink remaining |
| **Duck & Goose** | 165°F (74°C) | All parts |

**Why 165°F?** This temperature kills harmful bacteria like Salmonella instantly.

---

### Beef, Pork & Lamb

| Protein | Temperature | Doneness | Description |
|---------|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Beef Steaks & Roasts** | 120-125°F | Rare | Cool red center |
| | 130-135°F | Medium-Rare | Warm red center (recommended) |
| | 135-145°F | Medium | Warm pink center |
| | 145-155°F | Medium-Well | Slightly pink center |
| | 160°F+ | Well Done | No pink, fully cooked |
| **Ground Beef** | 160°F (71°C) | Well Done | **Required for safety** |
| **Pork Chops & Roasts** | 145°F (63°C) | Medium | Slightly pink, juicy |
| **Ground Pork** | 160°F (71°C) | Well Done | **Required for safety** |
| **Lamb Chops & Roasts** | 145°F (63°C) | Medium-Rare | Pink center (recommended) |
| **Ground Lamb** | 160°F (71°C) | Well Done | **Required for safety** |

**Rest Time:** After reaching temperature, let meat rest 3-5 minutes. Temperature will rise 5°F during rest.

---

### Fish & Seafood

| Protein | Minimum Temperature | Visual Cue |
|---------|-------------------|------------|
| **Fish (all types)** | 145°F (63°C) | Opaque, flakes easily with fork |
| **Shrimp, Lobster, Crab** | 145°F (63°C) | Flesh is pearly and opaque |
| **Clams, Oysters, Mussels** | Cook until shells open | Discard any that don't open |
| **Scallops** | 145°F (63°C) | Milky white or opaque, firm |

**Pro Tip:** Fish continues cooking after removal from heat. Remove at 140°F for perfect 145°F result.

---

### Other Proteins

| Protein | Minimum Temperature | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|--------|
| **Eggs (cooked)** | 160°F (71°C) | Yolk and white are firm |
| **Egg Dishes (casseroles)** | 160°F (71°C) | Center is firm |
| **Leftovers (reheating)** | 165°F (74°C) | Reheat thoroughly |
| **Ham (precooked)** | 140°F (60°C) | Just heating through |
| **Ham (raw)** | 145°F (63°C) | Follow package directions |

## Oven Temperature Guide

### Temperature Ranges by Cooking Method

| Temperature | Heat Level | Best For | Examples |
|-------------|------------|----------|----------|
| **200-250°F** | Very Low | Slow roasting, dehydrating | Jerky, dried tomatoes, low & slow brisket |
| **275-300°F** | Low | Slow cooking, braising | Pot roast, pulled pork, beans |
| **325-350°F** | Moderate | Baking, roasting | Cakes, cookies, roasted chicken |
| **375-400°F** | Medium-High | Standard roasting | Vegetables, fish, bread |
| **425-450°F** | High | Fast roasting, crisping | Roasted potatoes, pizza, chicken thighs |
| **475-500°F** | Very High | Searing, pizza | Pizza stone, crusty bread |
| **Broil (500°F+)** | Maximum | Browning, melting cheese | Finishing dishes, gratins |

## Stovetop Heat Levels

*Settings vary by stove type - these are general guidelines*

| Setting | Temp Range | Best For | Examples |
|---------|-----------|----------|----------|
| **Low (2-3)** | 200-300°F | Simmering, gentle cooking | Sauces, melting butter, warming |
| **Medium-Low (3-4)** | 300-325°F | Slow sautéing | Caramelizing onions, scrambled eggs |
| **Medium (4-6)** | 325-375°F | Standard sautéing, pan-frying | Grilled cheese, pancakes, vegetables |
| **Medium-High (6-7)** | 375-425°F | Searing, browning | Steaks, stir-fry, burgers |
| **High (8-10)** | 425-500°F+ | Fast searing, boiling water | Searing meat, boiling pasta water |

### Heat Test Tips

**Water Drop Test:**
- Water sizzles and evaporates slowly = Medium heat
- Water forms beads and dances = Medium-High heat
- Water instantly turns to steam = High heat

**Oil Shimmer Test:**
- Oil moves easily but doesn't smoke = Medium heat
- Oil shimmers with slight ripples = Medium-High heat (ideal for searing)
- Oil starts smoking = Too hot, reduce heat

## Grill Temperature Guide

### Direct vs Indirect Heat

| Temperature | Heat Level | Hand Test* | Best For |
|-------------|-----------|------------|----------|
| **250-300°F** | Low | 8-10 seconds | Slow smoking, delicate fish |
| **300-350°F** | Medium-Low | 6-7 seconds | Indirect roasting, whole chicken |
| **350-400°F** | Medium | 5-6 seconds | Standard grilling, vegetables |
| **400-450°F** | Medium-High | 3-4 seconds | Burgers, chicken pieces |
| **450-500°F** | High | 2-3 seconds | Steaks, quick-searing |
| **500°F+** | Very High | 1 second or less | Searing steaks, pizza |

***Hand Test:** Hold palm 5-6 inches above grill grate. Count how long you can keep it there comfortably.

## Deep Frying Temperature Guide

| Food Type | Oil Temperature | Notes |
|-----------|----------------|--------|
| **French Fries** | 325°F (first fry), 375°F (second fry) | Double-fry method for crispiness |
| **Chicken (breaded)** | 350-375°F | Maintain steady temperature |
| **Fish (battered)** | 350-365°F | Light and crispy |
| **Donuts** | 365-375°F | Too hot = burnt outside, raw inside |
| **Tempura** | 350-375°F | Quick fry for light, airy coating |

**Safety Tip:** Never fill oil more than halfway. Use a thermometer, not guesswork.

## Temperature Testing Tools

### Recommended Thermometers

1. **Instant-Read Thermometer** - Quick, accurate spot checks (3-5 seconds)
2. **Leave-In Probe Thermometer** - Monitors temperature while cooking
3. **Infrared Thermometer** - Surface temperature (griddles, pizza stones)

### How to Use a Meat Thermometer

1. Insert into **thickest part** of meat
2. Avoid touching bone, fat, or gristle
3. Wait 3-5 seconds for reading (instant-read)
4. Clean after each use

## Carryover Cooking

**What is it?** Meat continues cooking after removal from heat.

| Meat Type | Carryover Increase | Action |
|-----------|-------------------|--------|
| **Small cuts** (steaks, chops) | +5°F | Remove 5°F before target |
| **Large roasts** (whole chicken, prime rib) | +10-15°F | Remove 10°F before target |

**Example:** Want medium-rare steak (135°F)? Remove at 130°F, rest 5 minutes, perfect 135°F.

---

## Safety Reminders

✅ **Always use a food thermometer** - color and texture alone aren't reliable  
✅ **Clean thermometer** between uses to prevent cross-contamination  
✅ **Rest meat** after cooking - improves juiciness and safety  
✅ **Refrigerate leftovers** within 2 hours (1 hour if above 90°F outside)  
✅ **Reheat to 165°F** - leftovers must reach this temperature
