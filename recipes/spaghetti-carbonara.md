# Spaghetti Carbonara

## Description

This authentic Roman carbonara features silky pasta coated in a luxurious sauce made from eggs, pecorino cheese, and crispy guanciale. No cream needed—just simple, quality ingredients and proper technique create this iconic Italian dish.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 4 servings. Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Pasta

- 1 lb spaghetti
- Salt for pasta water

### Sauce & Protein

- 8 oz guanciale or pancetta, diced (or thick-cut bacon as substitute)
- 4 large eggs
- 1 cup pecorino Romano cheese, finely grated (plus more for serving)
- Freshly cracked black pepper (generous amount)
- Reserved pasta water

---

## Equipment

- Large pot for pasta
- Large skillet
- Large bowl
- Whisk
- Tongs
- Grater

---

## Instructions

### Prep Work (10 minutes)

1. **Bring water to boil:** Fill large pot with water, salt generously (should taste like sea water). Bring to boil.

2. **Dice guanciale:** Cut guanciale into 1/4-inch cubes.

3. **Make egg mixture:** In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, grated pecorino, and a generous amount of black pepper (at least 1 tsp). Set aside.

### Cooking (15 minutes)

4. **Cook guanciale:** Place diced guanciale in cold skillet. Turn heat to medium. Cook for 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until fat renders and meat is crispy. Remove from heat.

5. **Cook pasta:** Add spaghetti to boiling water. Cook until al dente (usually 1-2 minutes less than package directions). **Reserve 2 cups pasta water before draining.**

6. **Temper eggs:** Add 1/4 cup hot pasta water to egg mixture, whisking constantly. This prevents scrambling.

### Combine (Critical step!)

7. **Add pasta to pan:** Remove skillet from heat (very important!). Add drained pasta to guanciale pan. Toss to coat with rendered fat.

8. **Add egg mixture:** Working quickly off heat, pour egg mixture over pasta. Toss vigorously with tongs for 1-2 minutes. The residual heat will cook eggs into a creamy sauce.

9. **Adjust consistency:** Add reserved pasta water 1/4 cup at a time while tossing, until sauce reaches creamy consistency that coats pasta.

### Serve

10. **Plate immediately:** Divide among warm bowls. Top with extra pecorino and more black pepper. Serve right away—carbonara waits for no one!

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Off the heat:** This is crucial. Eggs should create a creamy sauce, not scramble. Add egg mixture to pasta off heat.
- **Toss vigorously:** Constant motion keeps eggs from scrambling and creates silky sauce.
- **Pasta water is magic:** The starchy water emulsifies the sauce. Don't skip it!
- **Timing matters:** Have everything ready. Once pasta is done, work quickly.

### Authentic vs. Substitutions

**Traditional:**
- Guanciale (cured pork jowl)
- Pecorino Romano cheese
- No cream, no garlic, no onions

**Acceptable substitutes:**
- Pancetta or thick-cut bacon for guanciale
- Parmesan for pecorino (or 50/50 mix)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Adding egg mixture to hot pan:** Results in scrambled eggs
- **Using cream:** Not traditional, and eggs provide all the creaminess needed
- **Not using pasta water:** Essential for proper sauce consistency
- **Letting it sit:** Carbonara is best served immediately

### Storage

- **Best fresh:** Carbonara doesn't store or reheat well.
- **If necessary:** Refrigerate for 1 day. Reheat gently with splash of water or broth.
