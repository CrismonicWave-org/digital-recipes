# Gluten-Free Chicken Parmesan

## Description

This is a gluten-free chicken parm built around a crunchy almond + parmesan “breading,” cooked in an air fryer (or baked), then topped with marinara. It’s paired with gluten-free pasta and a simple creamy garlic-nutmeg sauce.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 4 servings (about 1 chicken breast per person). Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Main Ingredients

- 4 chicken breasts (thin-cut preferred; butterfly if thick)
- 2 cups shredded parmesan cheese, finely chopped/minced after shredding
- 2 cups blanched almonds, pulsed to a coarse meal (not pure paste)
- 2 cups marinara sauce (organic if you prefer)
- 8 oz gluten-free pasta (about 4 “serving” portions; see package guidance)

### Binder / Coating

- 2 eggs, whisked (about 1 egg per 2 servings)

### Creamy Garlic-Nutmeg Sauce (Optional but recommended)

- 2 cups heavy whipping cream
- 4 tbsp butter (room temp if possible)
- 4 tbsp minced garlic (heaping)
- 4 tbsp ground nutmeg (heaping)

> Note: The original recipe lists nutmeg and garlic in very large amounts. If you want a more typical flavor balance, start with **1–2 tsp nutmeg total** and **1–2 tbsp garlic total**, then adjust.

---

## Equipment

- Air fryer (preferred) or oven + baking sheet
- Food processor / bullet grinder (for almonds)
- Shallow bowls/plates (for dredging station)
- Saucepan (if making the cream sauce)
- Instant-read thermometer (recommended)

---

## Instructions

### Prep Work (15 minutes)

1. **Prep chicken:** If frozen, thaw fully. If breasts are thick, butterfly or pound to an even thickness (about 1/2 inch is ideal).
2. **Make almond “crumbs”:** Pulse blanched almonds until they resemble a coarse meal.
3. **Make coating mix:** In a shallow dish, combine:
   - pulsed almonds
   - minced/finely chopped parmesan
4. **Egg wash:** In a second shallow dish, whisk the eggs.
5. **Set up station:** Chicken → egg → almond/parmesan mix → rack/plate.

### Coat Chicken (5 minutes)

6. **Bread chicken:** Dip each breast in egg, let excess drip off, then press firmly into the almond/parmesan mixture on both sides until well coated.

### Cook (Air Fryer) (12–18 minutes)

7. **Air fry:** Preheat air fryer to **375°F** if your model benefits from preheating.
8. **Cook chicken:** Spray basket lightly with oil (optional). Air fry **12–18 minutes**, flipping once halfway, until the thickest part reaches **165°F**.

### Oven Alternative (18–25 minutes)

9. **Bake:** Preheat oven to **425°F**. Place chicken on a lightly oiled rack over a baking sheet (best for crispness).
10. **Cook chicken:** Bake **18–25 minutes** (time varies by thickness), until **165°F** internal.

### Pasta + Sauce (while chicken cooks)

11. **Cook pasta:** Boil gluten-free pasta according to package directions. Drain.
12. **Make cream sauce (optional):**
    - In a saucepan over medium-low heat, melt butter.
    - Add garlic; cook 30–60 seconds until fragrant (don’t brown).
    - Add cream and nutmeg; gently simmer 3–5 minutes to slightly thicken.
    - Season to taste (salt optional; parmesan can be salty).

### Assemble & Serve (5 minutes)

13. **Top chicken:** Spoon warm marinara over each cooked chicken breast.
14. **Plate:** Serve chicken alongside (or over) gluten-free pasta. Spoon cream sauce over pasta (or drizzle lightly over chicken if desired).

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Crispier crust:** Use a rack (oven) or avoid overcrowding (air fryer) so air can circulate.
- **Thickness matters:** Even thickness prevents a burnt coating with undercooked center.
- **Thermometer wins:** Almond/parmesan coatings can brown quickly—temp is the best doneness check.

### Substitutions

- **Nut allergy:** Replace almonds with crushed gluten-free pork rinds or gluten-free breadcrumbs (if tolerated).
- **Dairy-free:** This recipe is parmesan/cream-forward; consider dairy-free parmesan + coconut cream, but results will vary.

### Storage

- **Refrigerate:** Store cooked chicken separately from sauced pasta for best texture; 3–4 days.
- **Reheat:** Air fryer or oven at 350°F until warmed through to re-crisp.
