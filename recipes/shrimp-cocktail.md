# Classic Shrimp Cocktail

## Description

This elegant appetizer features tender, perfectly cooked shrimp served chilled with zesty cocktail sauce. Simple to prepare yet impressive to serve, it's a timeless favorite for parties and special occasions.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 6 appetizer servings. Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Shrimp

- 2 lbs large shrimp (16-20 count), peeled and deveined, tails on
- 1 lemon, halved
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tbsp Old Bay seasoning
- 1 tbsp salt
- Ice for ice bath

### Cocktail Sauce

- 1 cup ketchup
- 3 tbsp prepared horseradish (adjust to taste)
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1/4 tsp hot sauce (optional)
- Pinch of salt

### For Serving

- Lemon wedges
- Fresh parsley for garnish
- Ice for serving platter (optional)

---

## Equipment

- Large pot
- Large bowl with ice water
- Colander
- Small bowl for sauce
- Serving platter

---

## Instructions

### Prep Work (5 minutes)

1. **Prepare ice bath:** Fill large bowl with ice and cold water. Set aside.

2. **Make cocktail sauce:** In small bowl, mix ketchup, horseradish, lemon juice, Worcestershire, and hot sauce. Taste and adjust horseradish level. Refrigerate until serving.

### Cook Shrimp (10 minutes)

3. **Boil water:** Fill large pot with water. Add lemon halves (squeeze juice in, then add rinds), bay leaves, Old Bay, and salt. Bring to rolling boil.

4. **Add shrimp:** Add shrimp to boiling water. Stir once.

5. **Cook briefly:** As soon as water returns to boil, cook for 2-3 minutes until shrimp are pink and just opaque. Don't overcook!

6. **Ice bath:** Immediately drain shrimp and plunge into ice bath. Let sit for 3-5 minutes to stop cooking and chill.

7. **Drain and refrigerate:** Drain shrimp well. Pat dry and refrigerate until serving time (at least 30 minutes).

### Serve

8. **Arrange platter:** If desired, fill serving platter with crushed ice. Arrange chilled shrimp around edge.

9. **Add sauce:** Place cocktail sauce in center bowl. Garnish with lemon wedges and parsley.

10. **Serve:** Guests can pick up shrimp by tail, dip in sauce, and enjoy!

---

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Don't overcook:** Shrimp cook FAST. They're done as soon as they turn pink and opaque. Overcooked shrimp are rubbery.
- **Ice bath is crucial:** Stops cooking immediately and ensures tender, juicy shrimp.
- **Buy good shrimp:** Look for wild-caught when possible. Frozen is fine—thaw overnight in fridge.
- **Adjust heat:** Love spicy? Add more horseradish and hot sauce. Prefer mild? Use less.

### Make Ahead

- **Shrimp:** Cook shrimp up to 24 hours ahead. Store refrigerated, covered.
- **Sauce:** Make sauce up to 3 days ahead. Refrigerate in airtight container.
- **Assembly:** Arrange platter up to 2 hours before serving. Keep refrigerated.

### Substitutions

- **Store-bought sauce:** Use prepared cocktail sauce to save time
- **No Old Bay:** Use paprika, celery salt, and black pepper
- **Larger shrimp:** Adjust cooking time—bigger shrimp need 30-60 seconds longer

### Variations

- **Cajun style:** Add cayenne and garlic to cooking water
- **Asian twist:** Serve with sweet chili sauce instead of cocktail sauce
- **Mexican:** Serve with chipotle mayo or salsa verde
