# Tuna Melt Sandwich

## Description

This classic American lunch features creamy tuna salad topped with melted cheddar cheese on toasted bread. Simple, satisfying, and ready in just 15 minutes—comfort food at its finest.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 2 sandwiches. Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Tuna Salad

- 2 (5 oz) cans tuna, drained
- 3 tbsp mayonnaise
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1/4 cup finely diced celery
- 2 tbsp finely diced red onion
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Sandwich Assembly

- 4 slices bread (sourdough, rye, or whole wheat)
- 4 slices cheddar cheese
- 2 tbsp butter, softened
- 2 slices tomato (optional)
- Lettuce leaves (optional)

---

## Equipment

- Medium bowl
- Fork
- Skillet or griddle
- Spatula

---

## Instructions

### Prep Work (10 minutes)

1. **Make tuna salad:** In medium bowl, break up drained tuna with fork. Add mayonnaise, mustard, celery, onion, and lemon juice. Mix until well combined. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

2. **Prep bread:** Butter one side of each bread slice.

### Assemble (2 minutes)

3. **Build sandwiches:** Place 2 slices bread butter-side down. Divide tuna salad between the two slices, spreading evenly. Add tomato slice if using.

4. **Add cheese:** Top each with 2 slices of cheese. Place remaining bread slices on top, butter-side up.

### Cook (5 minutes)

5. **Heat pan:** Preheat skillet or griddle to medium heat.

6. **Grill sandwiches:** Place sandwiches in pan. Cook for 2-3 minutes until bottom is golden brown and crispy.

7. **Flip:** Carefully flip sandwiches with spatula. Cook another 2-3 minutes until second side is golden and cheese is melted.

8. **Press gently:** Use spatula to gently press down on sandwiches to help cheese melt evenly.

### Serve

9. **Cut and serve:** Cut sandwiches in half diagonally. Serve immediately while cheese is hot and melty.

---

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Drain tuna well:** Excess liquid makes soggy sandwiches. Press tuna in strainer to remove extra moisture.
- **Room temperature tuna:** Takes mayonnaise better than cold tuna straight from fridge.
- **Medium heat:** Too hot and bread burns before cheese melts. Medium heat is perfect.
- **Cover pan:** Place lid over sandwiches for 30 seconds to help cheese melt faster.

### Tuna Salad Variations

- **Classic:** Add chopped hard-boiled egg and dill pickle
- **Mediterranean:** Add kalamata olives and sun-dried tomatoes
- **Spicy:** Add diced jalapeño and pepper jack cheese
- **Herby:** Add fresh dill and parsley

### Substitutions

- **Gluten-free:** Use gluten-free bread
- **Lighter:** Use light mayo or Greek yogurt instead of regular mayo
- **Different cheese:** Try Swiss, provolone, or pepper jack
- **Open-faced:** Use one slice of bread per serving, broil to melt cheese

### Serving Suggestions

- **Sides:** Potato chips, pickle spears, fresh fruit
- **Soup pairing:** Tomato soup is classic
- **Salad:** Side salad with vinaigrette

### Storage

- **Tuna salad:** Make ahead and refrigerate for up to 3 days
- **Cooked sandwiches:** Best eaten immediately. Tuna melt doesn't store well once assembled and cooked.
