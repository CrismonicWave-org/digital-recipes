# Chicken Tikka Masala

## Description

This beloved Indian dish features tender chicken pieces in a velvety tomato-cream sauce infused with warm spices. Rich, aromatic, and absolutely delicious served over basmati rice with naan bread.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 6 servings. Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Chicken Marinade

- 2 lbs boneless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces
- 1 cup plain yogurt (full-fat preferred)
- 2 tbsp lemon juice
- 2 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
- 1 tsp salt

### Masala Sauce

- 3 tbsp butter or ghee
- 1 large onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced
- 2 tsp garam masala
- 2 tsp ground cumin
- 2 tsp paprika
- 1 tsp turmeric
- 1/2 tsp cayenne (adjust to taste)
- 1 (28 oz) can crushed tomatoes
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tbsp sugar
- Salt to taste
- Fresh cilantro for garnish

---

## Equipment

- Large bowl for marinating
- Baking sheet or broiler pan
- Large saucepan or Dutch oven
- Wooden spoon

---

## Instructions

### Marinate Chicken (20 minutes minimum, or overnight)

1. **Make marinade:** In a large bowl, combine yogurt, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin, paprika, cayenne, and salt.

2. **Marinate chicken:** Add chicken pieces to marinade. Toss to coat thoroughly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 20 minutes (or up to 24 hours for best flavor).

### Cook Chicken (10-12 minutes)

3. **Preheat broiler:** Set oven to broil on high. Line baking sheet with foil.

4. **Arrange chicken:** Spread marinated chicken in single layer on baking sheet. Discard excess marinade.

5. **Broil:** Broil for 10-12 minutes, turning once halfway through, until chicken is slightly charred and cooked through. Set aside.

### Make Sauce (15-20 minutes)

6. **Sauté aromatics:** In large saucepan over medium heat, melt butter. Add onion and cook for 5-6 minutes until softened and lightly golden.

7. **Add garlic and ginger:** Add garlic and ginger. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.

8. **Add spices:** Add garam masala, cumin, paprika, turmeric, and cayenne. Toast for 30-60 seconds, stirring constantly.

9. **Add tomatoes:** Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and sugar. Simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.

10. **Blend (optional):** For smoother sauce, use immersion blender or transfer to regular blender and puree until smooth. Return to pan.

11. **Add cream:** Stir in heavy cream. Simmer for 5 minutes. Season with salt to taste.

12. **Add chicken:** Add broiled chicken to sauce. Simmer for 5 minutes to let flavors meld.

### Serve

13. **Garnish and serve:** Garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve over basmati rice with warm naan bread.

---

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Marinate longer:** Overnight marination gives best flavor and tenderness.
- **Don't skip the char:** Broiling adds authentic smoky flavor. Grill is even better if available!
- **Adjust heat:** Control spice level with cayenne amount. This recipe is mild-medium.
- **Full-fat dairy:** Use full-fat yogurt and cream for best flavor and texture.

### Substitutions

- **Dairy-free:** Use coconut yogurt for marinade, coconut cream for sauce
- **Protein options:** Substitute paneer, tofu, or chickpeas for vegetarian version
- **Spice alternatives:** Don't have all spices? Curry powder can substitute in a pinch

### Serving Suggestions

- **Rice:** Basmati rice or naan bread
- **Sides:** Cucumber raita, mango chutney, pickled onions
- **Garnish:** Fresh cilantro, squeeze of lime

### Storage

- **Refrigerate:** Store in airtight container for 3-4 days. Flavors improve overnight!
- **Freeze:** Freezes excellently for up to 3 months.
- **Reheat:** Stovetop over low heat, adding splash of cream if needed.
