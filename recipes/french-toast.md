# Classic French Toast

## Description

This classic French toast (pain perdu) features thick slices of bread soaked in a rich egg custard, then cooked until golden and crispy. Simple, elegant, and utterly delicious with maple syrup.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 8 slices (4 servings). Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Main Ingredients

- 8 slices thick-cut bread (brioche, challah, or Texas toast)
- 4 large eggs
- 1 cup whole milk
- 2 tbsp granulated sugar
- 1 tsp vanilla extract
- 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
- Pinch of salt
- 3 tbsp butter (for cooking)

### For Serving

- Maple syrup
- Powdered sugar
- Fresh berries
- Whipped cream (optional)

---

## Equipment

- Shallow dish or pie plate
- Whisk
- Griddle or large skillet
- Spatula
- Plate for finished toast

---

## Instructions

### Prep Work (5 minutes)

1. **Make custard:** In a shallow dish, whisk together eggs, milk, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, and salt until well combined.

2. **Prep bread:** If using fresh bread, let slices sit out for 10-15 minutes to dry slightly. Day-old bread works best.

### Cooking (10 minutes)

3. **Heat pan:** Preheat griddle or skillet to medium heat (325-350°F). Add 1 tbsp butter.

4. **Soak bread:** Dip one slice of bread into custard mixture, turning to coat both sides. Let soak for 5-10 seconds per side. Don't oversoak or bread will fall apart.

5. **Cook first side:** Place soaked bread on hot griddle. Cook for 2-3 minutes until golden brown on bottom.

6. **Flip and finish:** Flip carefully with spatula. Cook another 2-3 minutes until second side is golden and center is cooked through.

7. **Repeat:** Add more butter as needed and continue with remaining slices.

### Serve

8. **Plate:** Stack 2 slices per serving. Dust with powdered sugar, top with berries, and drizzle with warm maple syrup.

---

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Day-old bread is best:** Fresh bread gets too soggy. Day-old or lightly toasted bread holds up better.
- **Don't oversoak:** Quick dip (5-10 seconds per side) prevents soggy centers.
- **Medium heat:** Too hot and outside burns before inside cooks. Too low and it won't get crispy.
- **Bread choice matters:** Brioche and challah are richest. Texas toast is classic. Avoid thin sandwich bread.

### Substitutions

- **Dairy-free:** Use almond milk or oat milk, and coconut oil instead of butter.
- **Lower sugar:** Reduce or omit sugar in custard, rely on syrup for sweetness.
- **Gluten-free:** Use thick-cut gluten-free bread.

### Variations

- **Stuffed:** Make a cream cheese or Nutella sandwich before dipping.
- **Savory:** Omit sugar and cinnamon, add black pepper and herbs. Serve with bacon.

### Storage

- **Refrigerate:** Store cooked French toast for 2-3 days.
- **Freeze:** Layer between parchment, freeze up to 2 months.
- **Reheat:** Toaster or 350°F oven for 5-7 minutes.
