# Classic Tiramisu

## Description

This authentic Italian tiramisu features layers of espresso-soaked ladyfinger cookies and a rich mascarpone cream, dusted with cocoa powder. Meaning "pick me up" in Italian, this no-bake dessert is perfect for entertaining and tastes even better the next day.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes one 9x13 pan (12 servings). Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Mascarpone Cream

- 6 large egg yolks
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar
- 1 1/4 cups heavy cream
- 2 cups (16 oz) mascarpone cheese, room temperature
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

### Coffee Layer

- 2 cups strong brewed espresso or coffee, cooled
- 3 tbsp coffee liqueur (Kahlúa) or rum (optional)
- 2 tbsp granulated sugar

### Assembly

- 40-48 ladyfinger cookies (savoiardi)
- 2 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder for dusting
- Dark chocolate shavings for garnish (optional)

---

## Equipment

- 9x13 inch dish
- Double boiler or heatproof bowl over simmering water
- Electric mixer
- Shallow dish for coffee dipping
- Fine mesh sieve for dusting cocoa

---

## Instructions

### Make Mascarpone Cream (15 minutes)

1. **Make zabaglione:** In heatproof bowl set over simmering water (double boiler), whisk egg yolks and sugar constantly for 8-10 minutes until thick, pale, and tripled in volume. Mixture should reach 160°F for food safety.

2. **Cool:** Remove from heat. Let cool to room temperature, stirring occasionally (about 10 minutes).

3. **Whip cream:** In separate bowl, whip heavy cream to stiff peaks.

4. **Mix mascarpone:** In another bowl, beat mascarpone with vanilla until smooth and creamy.

5. **Combine:** Gently fold cooled zabaglione into mascarpone until smooth. Then fold in whipped cream in two additions until no streaks remain.

### Prepare Coffee (5 minutes)

6. **Mix coffee:** In shallow dish, combine cooled espresso, liqueur (if using), and sugar. Stir until sugar dissolves.

### Assemble (10 minutes)

7. **First layer:** Quickly dip each ladyfinger in coffee mixture (1-2 seconds per side—don't oversoak!). Arrange in single layer in 9x13 dish, breaking fingers to fit if needed.

8. **Add cream:** Spread half of mascarpone cream evenly over ladyfingers.

9. **Second layer:** Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers.

10. **Top cream:** Spread remaining mascarpone cream over top layer, smoothing with spatula.

11. **Chill:** Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours (overnight is best for flavors to meld).

### Serve

12. **Dust with cocoa:** Just before serving, dust generously with cocoa powder using fine mesh sieve. Add chocolate shavings if desired.

13. **Cut and serve:** Cut into squares and serve cold.

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Don't oversoak:** Quick dip only! Soggy ladyfingers make watery tiramisu.
- **Room temperature mascarpone:** Prevents lumps in cream.
- **Make ahead:** Tiramisu tastes better after 12-24 hours in fridge. Flavors meld beautifully.
- **Zabaglione temp:** Heating eggs to 160°F makes them safe to eat without further cooking.

### No Raw Eggs Version

Use pasteurized eggs, or substitute zabaglione with this method:
- Beat mascarpone with 3/4 cup powdered sugar and 1 tsp vanilla
- Fold in whipped cream
- Skip egg yolk step entirely (slightly different texture but still delicious)

### Substitutions

- **No mascarpone:** Mix 16 oz cream cheese + 1/2 cup heavy cream (not as rich but works)
- **No coffee liqueur:** Use 1 tsp rum extract or omit entirely
- **Gluten-free:** Use gluten-free ladyfingers
- **Decaf:** Use decaf espresso or strong decaf coffee

### Variations

- **Chocolate tiramisu:** Add 2 tbsp cocoa powder to mascarpone cream
- **Berry tiramisu:** Add layer of fresh berries between layers
- **Amaretto version:** Use amaretto instead of coffee liqueur

### Storage

- **Refrigerate:** Cover and store for 3-4 days
- **Don't freeze:** Cream-based desserts don't freeze well
