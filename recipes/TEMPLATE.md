## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 4 servings. Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Main Ingredients

- 1 lb ground beef (80/20 blend)
- 4 burger buns
- 4 slices cheese (cheddar, American, or your choice)
- 1/2 cup diced onions
- 2 large tomatoes, sliced

### Seasonings & Condiments

- 2 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp black pepper, freshly ground
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1/4 cup ketchup
- 2 tbsp mayonnaise
- 1 tbsp mustard

### Optional Garnishes

- Lettuce leaves
- Pickle slices
- Fresh herbs (cilantro, parsley)

## Instructions

### Prep Work (15 minutes)

1. **Prepare ingredients:** Dice the onions into 1/4-inch pieces. Slice tomatoes into 1/4-inch rounds. Let ground beef come to room temperature (15-20 minutes before cooking).

2. **Mix seasonings:** In a small bowl, combine salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Set aside.

3. **Set up your station:** Arrange all ingredients within easy reach. Have a clean plate ready for cooked items.

### Cooking (15 minutes)

4. **Preheat pan:** Heat cast iron skillet to medium-high heat (375-400°F). Add a light coating of oil.

5. **Form patties:** Divide beef into 4 equal portions (4 oz each). Form into loose balls - do NOT overwork the meat.

6. **Cook and season:** Place beef balls on hot skillet. Using spatula, press firmly to create thin patties (about 1/3 inch thick). Season immediately with salt mixture.

7. **First flip:** Cook for 2-3 minutes until edges are crispy and brown. Flip once. Season the cooked side.

8. **Add cheese:** Place one slice of cheese on each patty. Cook for 2 more minutes until cheese melts and internal temperature reaches 160°F.

9. **Toast buns:** While burgers finish, place buns cut-side down on griddle for 30-60 seconds until lightly toasted.

### Assembly

10. **Build your burger:** Spread mayo and mustard on bottom bun. Add lettuce, then patty with melted cheese, then tomato, onions, and pickles. Top with upper bun.

11. **Serve immediately** while hot and cheese is melted.

---

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Don't press the patty again** after the initial smash - you'll squeeze out all the juices!
- **Single flip rule:** Flip once and only once for the best crust.
- **Cheese timing:** Add cheese with 2 minutes remaining so it melts perfectly without burning.

### Substitutions

- **Gluten-free:** Use gluten-free buns or lettuce wraps
- **Dairy-free:** Skip the cheese or use dairy-free alternative
- **Leaner meat:** Use 90/10 ground beef, but burger will be less juicy

### Storage

- **Refrigerate:** Cooked patties keep for 3-4 days in airtight container
- **Freeze:** Wrap cooked patties individually, freeze up to 3 months
- **Reheat:** Best in skillet over medium heat, or 30 seconds in microwave
