# Classic Beef Tacos

## Description

These classic beef tacos feature perfectly seasoned ground beef in crispy corn shells, topped with fresh lettuce, tomatoes, cheese, and all your favorite fixings. Quick, easy, and always a crowd-pleaser.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 12 tacos (4 servings, 3 tacos each). Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Meat Filling

- 1 lb ground beef (80/20 blend)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1/2 cup diced onion
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp taco seasoning (store-bought or homemade)
- 1/2 cup water
- Salt to taste

### Taco Shells & Toppings

- 12 taco shells (hard corn or soft flour tortillas)
- 2 cups shredded lettuce
- 1 cup diced tomatoes
- 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 1/2 cup salsa
- Sliced jalapeños (optional)
- Fresh cilantro (optional)
- Lime wedges

---

## Equipment

- Large skillet
- Wooden spoon or spatula
- Cutting board and knife
- Baking sheet (for warming shells)

---

## Instructions

### Prep Work (10 minutes)

1. **Prep toppings:** Shred lettuce, dice tomatoes, shred cheese, and arrange in serving bowls.

2. **Dice onion and garlic:** Finely dice onion and mince garlic.

### Cooking (15 minutes)

3. **Brown beef:** Heat olive oil in large skillet over medium-high heat. Add ground beef, breaking it up with wooden spoon. Cook for 5-6 minutes until browned and no pink remains.

4. **Add aromatics:** Drain excess fat if needed. Add diced onion and garlic. Cook for 2-3 minutes until onion is softened.

5. **Season:** Add taco seasoning and water. Stir well to combine. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 5-7 minutes until liquid is mostly absorbed and meat is well coated. Taste and adjust salt.

6. **Warm shells:** While meat simmers, warm taco shells in 350°F oven for 5 minutes, or according to package directions.

### Assembly

7. **Build tacos:** Fill each taco shell with 2-3 tbsp seasoned beef.

8. **Add toppings:** Top with lettuce, tomatoes, cheese, sour cream, salsa, and any optional toppings.

9. **Serve:** Serve immediately with lime wedges on the side.

---

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Don't overdrain:** Save a little fat for flavor, just remove excess.
- **Season in layers:** Taste and adjust seasoning after adding taco spice.
- **Warm shells properly:** Cold shells crack easily. Warm shells are pliable and hold together better.

### Homemade Taco Seasoning

Mix together:
- 1 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp onion powder
- 1/4 tsp cayenne (optional)
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Substitutions

- **Protein options:** Ground turkey, chicken, or plant-based crumbles
- **Gluten-free:** Ensure taco seasoning is gluten-free, use corn tortillas
- **Dairy-free:** Skip cheese and sour cream, or use dairy-free alternatives
- **Lower fat:** Use 90/10 ground beef or ground turkey

### Storage

- **Refrigerate:** Store cooked beef filling separately from shells and toppings. Reheat beef in skillet. Use within 3-4 days.
- **Freeze:** Cooked beef freezes well for up to 3 months.
