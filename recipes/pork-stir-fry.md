# Pork Stir-Fry with Vegetables

## Description

Tender slices of pork and colorful vegetables tossed in a savory stir-fry sauce. This quick and easy dinner delivers bold flavors and satisfying textures, perfect served over rice or noodles.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 4 servings. Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Pork

- 1 lb pork tenderloin or pork loin, thinly sliced
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp cornstarch
- 1 tsp sesame oil

### Vegetables

- 2 cups broccoli florets
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup snap peas
- 1 medium carrot, julienned
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, minced

### Stir-Fry Sauce

- 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 2 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tbsp honey or brown sugar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1/2 cup chicken broth
- 1 tbsp cornstarch

### For Cooking

- 3 tbsp vegetable oil (divided)
- Cooked rice for serving
- Sesame seeds for garnish
- Sliced green onions for garnish

---

## Equipment

- Large wok or skillet
- Small bowl for sauce
- Cutting board and sharp knife
- Wooden spoon or spatula

---

## Instructions

### Prep Work (15 minutes)

1. **Slice pork:** Cut pork against the grain into thin 1/4-inch slices. Place in bowl.

2. **Marinate pork:** Add 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp cornstarch, and 1 tsp sesame oil to pork. Toss to coat. Let sit for 10 minutes.

3. **Prep vegetables:** Cut all vegetables into uniform bite-sized pieces. Keep garlic and ginger separate.

4. **Make sauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, oyster sauce, rice vinegar, honey, sesame oil, chicken broth, and cornstarch. Set aside.

### Cooking (10 minutes)

5. **Heat wok:** Heat wok or large skillet over high heat until smoking hot. Add 2 tbsp vegetable oil.

6. **Cook pork:** Add marinated pork in a single layer. Let sear for 1-2 minutes without stirring. Toss and cook another 1-2 minutes until just cooked through. Remove to a plate.

7. **Cook vegetables:** Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to wok. Add broccoli and carrots first. Stir-fry for 2 minutes.

8. **Add remaining vegetables:** Add bell pepper and snap peas. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until crisp-tender.

9. **Add aromatics:** Push vegetables to sides of wok. Add garlic and ginger to center. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.

10. **Combine:** Return pork to wok. Give sauce a quick stir (cornstarch settles), then pour over everything. Toss for 1-2 minutes until sauce thickens and coats everything.

### Serve

11. **Plate:** Serve immediately over hot rice. Garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.

---

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **High heat is key:** Wok cooking requires very high heat for proper sear and crisp vegetables.
- **Don't overcrowd:** Cook in batches if needed. Overcrowding steams instead of sears.
- **Prep everything first:** Stir-frying moves fast. Have all ingredients ready before you start cooking.
- **Slice pork thin:** Freeze for 30 minutes to make slicing easier.

### Substitutions

- **Protein:** Substitute chicken, beef, shrimp, or tofu
- **Vegetables:** Use any combination—snow peas, bok choy, mushrooms, zucchini, baby corn
- **Gluten-free:** Use tamari instead of soy sauce, and ensure oyster sauce is GF
- **Spicy version:** Add 1 tsp chili garlic sauce or red pepper flakes

### Storage

- **Refrigerate:** Store in airtight container for 3-4 days.
- **Reheat:** Wok or skillet over high heat is best. Microwave works but vegetables will be softer.
- **Freeze:** Not recommended—vegetables lose texture when frozen.
