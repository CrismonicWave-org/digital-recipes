# Classic Buttermilk Pancakes

## Description

These fluffy buttermilk pancakes are the ultimate breakfast comfort food. With a tender, airy texture and slightly tangy flavor from the buttermilk, they're perfect with maple syrup and butter.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 12-14 medium pancakes (4 servings). Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Dry Ingredients

- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 2 tbsp granulated sugar
- 2 tsp baking powder
- 1 tsp baking soda
- 1/2 tsp salt

### Wet Ingredients

- 2 cups buttermilk
- 2 large eggs
- 1/4 cup melted butter (plus more for cooking)
- 1 tsp vanilla extract

### For Serving

- Maple syrup
- Butter pats
- Fresh berries (optional)
- Powdered sugar (optional)

---

## Equipment

- Large mixing bowl
- Medium mixing bowl
- Whisk
- Measuring cups and spoons
- Griddle or large nonstick skillet
- 1/4 cup measuring cup or ladle
- Spatula

---

## Instructions

### Prep Work (10 minutes)

1. **Mix dry ingredients:** In a large bowl, whisk together flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt until well combined.

2. **Mix wet ingredients:** In a medium bowl, whisk together buttermilk, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla extract until smooth.

3. **Combine:** Pour wet ingredients into dry ingredients. Gently fold together with a spatula until just combined. Some lumps are okay—don't overmix! Let batter rest for 5 minutes.

### Cooking (10 minutes)

4. **Heat griddle:** Preheat griddle or skillet to medium heat (350°F). Add a small pat of butter to coat the surface.

5. **Test temperature:** Drop a tiny bit of batter on the griddle. It should sizzle gently. If it browns immediately, reduce heat.

6. **Cook pancakes:** Pour 1/4 cup batter per pancake onto griddle. Cook until bubbles form on surface and edges look set (2-3 minutes).

7. **Flip:** When bubbles pop and don't fill back in, flip pancakes. Cook another 1-2 minutes until golden brown.

8. **Keep warm:** Transfer cooked pancakes to a plate and cover with foil to keep warm while you cook remaining batter.

### Serve

9. **Stack and serve:** Stack 3-4 pancakes per serving. Top with butter and drizzle with warm maple syrup. Add berries or powdered sugar if desired.

---

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Don't overmix:** Lumpy batter = fluffy pancakes. Overmixed batter = tough pancakes.
- **Rest the batter:** 5 minutes lets the flour hydrate and gives fluffier results.
- **Consistent heat:** Medium heat prevents burning outside while ensuring cooked-through centers.
- **Fresh baking powder:** Check expiration date for maximum fluff.

### Substitutions

- **No buttermilk:** Use 2 cups milk + 2 tbsp white vinegar or lemon juice. Let sit 5 minutes.
- **Gluten-free:** Use 1:1 gluten-free flour blend. May need slightly more liquid.
- **Dairy-free:** Use non-dairy milk + vinegar, and coconut oil instead of butter.

### Storage

- **Refrigerate:** Store cooked pancakes in airtight container for 3-4 days.
- **Freeze:** Layer between parchment paper, freeze up to 2 months.
- **Reheat:** Toaster, microwave (30 seconds), or warm skillet.
