# Herb-Crusted Grilled Salmon

## Description

This simple yet elegant grilled salmon features a flavorful herb crust with fresh dill, parsley, and garlic, finished with a squeeze of lemon. Light, healthy, and restaurant-quality in just 20 minutes.

---

## Ingredients

> **Serving Size:** This recipe makes 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each). Scale ingredients accordingly.

### Salmon

- 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each, skin-on)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and pepper to taste

### Herb Crust

- 2 tbsp fresh dill, chopped (or 2 tsp dried)
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp lemon zest
- 1 tsp paprika
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp salt

### For Serving

- 2 lemons, cut into wedges
- Fresh herbs for garnish

---

## Equipment

- Grill or grill pan
- Small bowl
- Brush or spoon for oil
- Spatula
- Instant-read thermometer (optional)

---

## Instructions

### Prep Work (10 minutes)

1. **Pat salmon dry:** Use paper towels to pat fillets completely dry. This ensures a good sear.

2. **Make herb mixture:** In a small bowl, combine dill, parsley, garlic, lemon zest, paprika, pepper, and salt.

3. **Prep grill:** Preheat grill to medium-high heat (400°F). Clean and oil grates well to prevent sticking.

### Cooking (10 minutes)

4. **Season salmon:** Brush both sides of salmon with olive oil. Season flesh side generously with herb mixture. Season skin side with just salt and pepper.

5. **Grill skin-side down:** Place salmon skin-side down on grill. Close lid. Grill for 6-8 minutes without moving.

6. **Check for release:** Salmon should release easily from grill when ready to flip. If it sticks, cook 1-2 minutes longer.

7. **Flip carefully:** Using a wide spatula, carefully flip salmon. Grill herb-side down for 1-2 minutes until internal temperature reaches 145°F (medium) or 125°F (medium-rare).

8. **Rest:** Remove from grill and let rest for 2 minutes.

### Serve

9. **Plate:** Serve salmon with lemon wedges and garnish with fresh herbs. Pair with roasted vegetables or rice.

---

## Chef's Notes

### Pro Tips

- **Skin-on is key:** Helps prevent sticking and keeps fish moist. You can remove after cooking if preferred.
- **Don't flip too early:** Salmon will release when it's ready. Forcing it will tear the fish.
- **Temperature guide:** 125°F = medium-rare (moist, slightly translucent), 145°F = medium (USDA recommended)
- **Oil the grates:** Prevents sticking. Use a paper towel dipped in oil, held with tongs.

### Oven Alternative

Preheat oven to 400°F. Place salmon on oiled baking sheet. Bake for 12-15 minutes until desired doneness.

### Substitutions

- **Fresh herbs:** Use any combination of dill, parsley, cilantro, basil, or thyme
- **Spice it up:** Add cayenne or red pepper flakes to herb mixture
- **Gluten-free / Dairy-free:** This recipe is naturally both!

### Storage

- **Refrigerate:** Store cooked salmon for 2-3 days in airtight container.
- **Reheat:** Oven at 275°F for 10-12 minutes, or serve cold on salad.
- **Don't freeze:** Cooked salmon doesn't freeze well.
