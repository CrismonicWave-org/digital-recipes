# US Measurement Conversions

**📏 All RecipesGalore recipes use US standard measurements for consistency.**

This guide helps you understand US cooking measurements and convert between units.

## Volume Measurements

### Basic Conversions

| Unit | Equals | Common Use |
|------|--------|------------|
| 1 cup (c) | 8 fluid ounces (fl oz) | Liquids, flour, sugar |
| 1 cup | 16 tablespoons (tbsp) | Dry and liquid ingredients |
| 1/2 cup | 4 fl oz | 8 tbsp |
| 1/3 cup | 2.67 fl oz | 5 tbsp + 1 tsp |
| 1/4 cup | 2 fl oz | 4 tbsp |
| 1 tablespoon (tbsp) | 3 teaspoons (tsp) | Spices, oils, extracts |
| 1 tablespoon | 1/2 fluid ounce | Small liquid amounts |
| 1 teaspoon (tsp) | 1/3 tablespoon | Small dry/liquid amounts |

## Measuring Tips

### Dry Ingredients

- **Flour:** Spoon into measuring cup, level off with knife (don't scoop or pack)
- **Brown Sugar:** Pack firmly into measuring cup, level off
- **Granulated Sugar:** Spoon and level, or scoop gently and level

### Liquid Ingredients

- Use clear measuring cups with pour spouts
- Place on flat surface, view at eye level
- Fill to exact line

### Sticky Ingredients

- **Honey/Molasses:** Spray measuring cup with cooking spray first for easy release
- **Peanut Butter:** Pack firmly into measuring cup, level off
- **Shortening:** Pack firmly, level off

### Spices & Small Amounts

- Fill spoon to brim, level off with knife
- "Heaping" = rounded mound above rim
- "Scant" = slightly less than level

## Quick Reference: Common Amounts

### "A Pinch"
- Between 1/16 and 1/8 teaspoon
- What you can pinch between thumb and forefinger

### "A Dash"
- About 1/8 teaspoon
- 2-3 drops of liquid

### "A Handful"
- Varies by hand size and ingredient
- Generally 1/4 to 1/2 cup

### "To Taste"
- Season gradually, taste, adjust
- No specific measurement

## Metric Equivalents (For Reference)

*Most US recipes don't use metric, but here's a quick guide*

### Volume
- 1 cup = 240 milliliters (ml)
- 1 tablespoon = 15 ml
- 1 teaspoon = 5 ml
- 1 fluid ounce = 30 ml

### Weight
- 1 ounce = 28 grams (g)
- 1 pound = 454 grams (g) or 0.45 kilograms (kg)
