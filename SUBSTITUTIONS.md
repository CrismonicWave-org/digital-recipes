# Common Ingredient Substitutions

**🔄 No ingredient? No problem! Use these tested substitutions to keep cooking.**

This guide helps you substitute ingredients based on what you have on hand, dietary restrictions, or flavor preferences.

---

## Dairy Substitutions

### Milk

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **Whole Milk** | 1 cup | 1 cup 2% or skim milk + 1 tbsp melted butter | Adds back fat content |
| | | 1 cup almond/soy/oat milk | Dairy-free option |
| | | 1/2 cup evaporated milk + 1/2 cup water | Richer flavor |
| **Buttermilk** | 1 cup | 1 cup milk + 1 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar | Let sit 5-10 minutes |
| | | 1 cup plain yogurt thinned with milk | Tangier flavor |
| **Heavy Cream** | 1 cup | 3/4 cup milk + 1/4 cup melted butter | Not for whipping |
| | | 1 cup coconut cream (chilled) | Dairy-free, slight coconut flavor |
| **Sour Cream** | 1 cup | 1 cup plain Greek yogurt | Healthier, similar tang |
| | | 1 cup cottage cheese blended smooth | Milder flavor |

### Butter

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **Butter** | 1 cup (2 sticks) | 1 cup margarine | Similar texture |
| | | 3/4 cup vegetable oil | Use 3/4 amount, affects texture |
| | | 3/4 cup coconut oil | Dairy-free, slight coconut flavor |
| | | 1 cup applesauce (baking only) | Lower fat, denser result |
| **Salted Butter** | 1 cup | 1 cup unsalted butter + 1/2 tsp salt | Control sodium |

### Cheese

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **Parmesan** | 1 cup grated | 1 cup Pecorino Romano | Saltier, sharper |
| | | 1 cup Asiago | Milder, nutty flavor |
| **Cheddar** | 1 cup shredded | 1 cup Colby or Monterey Jack | Milder flavor |
| | | 1 cup Gouda | Smokier, creamier |
| **Mozzarella** | 1 cup shredded | 1 cup Monterey Jack | Similar melt |
| | | 1 cup provolone | Slightly stronger flavor |
| **Cream Cheese** | 8 oz | 8 oz Neufchâtel cheese | Lower fat, similar texture |
| | | 1 cup Greek yogurt (strained overnight) | Tangier, healthier |

## Egg Substitutions

### Whole Eggs (for binding/moisture)

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Best For |
|----------|--------|------------|----------|
| **1 Egg** | 1 large | 1/4 cup applesauce | Baking (cakes, muffins) |
| | | 1/4 cup mashed banana | Baking (adds banana flavor) |
| | | 1/4 cup silken tofu (blended) | Vegan baking, dense texture |
| | | 1 tbsp ground flaxseed + 3 tbsp water | Vegan, nutty flavor (let sit 5 min) |
| | | 1 tbsp chia seeds + 3 tbsp water | Vegan, mild flavor (let sit 5 min) |
| | | 3 tbsp aquafaba (chickpea liquid) | Vegan, good for meringues |

### Egg Whites (for leavening)

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **1 Egg White** | ~2 tbsp | 2 tbsp aquafaba | Can be whipped like egg whites |
| | | 1 tbsp gelatin + 3 tbsp water | Let bloom, then heat |

**Note:** Eggs have unique properties. Substitutes work best in recipes where eggs aren't the star ingredient.

## Flour & Grain Substitutions

### All-Purpose Flour

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **All-Purpose Flour** | 1 cup | 1 cup + 2 tbsp cake flour | Lighter, more delicate |
| | | 1 cup - 2 tbsp bread flour | Chewier, more structure |
| | | 1 cup whole wheat flour | Denser, nuttier (use 7/8 cup) |
| | | 1 cup gluten-free 1:1 blend | Follow package instructions |
| **Cake Flour** | 1 cup | 3/4 cup + 2 tbsp all-purpose flour + 2 tbsp cornstarch | Lighter texture |
| **Bread Flour** | 1 cup | 1 cup all-purpose flour + 1 tsp vital wheat gluten | More protein/chewiness |
| **Self-Rising Flour** | 1 cup | 1 cup all-purpose flour + 1½ tsp baking powder + 1/4 tsp salt | Mix well |

### Gluten-Free Options

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **1 cup Wheat Flour** | 1 cup | 1 cup almond flour | Dense, nutty (best for cookies) |
| | | 3/4 cup coconut flour + extra liquid | Very absorbent, use less |
| | | 1 cup oat flour | Mild flavor, slightly dense |
| | | 1 cup rice flour | Neutral, slightly gritty |
| | | 1 cup gluten-free flour blend | Best 1:1 substitute |

## Sugar Substitutions

### Granulated Sugar

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **Granulated Sugar** | 1 cup | 1 cup brown sugar (packed) | Moister, caramel flavor |
| | | 3/4 cup honey + reduce liquid by 1/4 cup | Sweeter, affects texture |

| | | 1 cup coconut sugar | Similar moisture, slight coconut flavor |

## Oil & Fat Substitutions

### Cooking Oils

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **Vegetable Oil** | 1 cup | 1 cup canola oil | Neutral flavor |
| | | 1 cup melted coconut oil | Slight coconut flavor |
| | | 1 cup olive oil | Stronger flavor (best for savory) |
| | | 3/4 cup applesauce (baking only) | Lower fat, denser texture |
| **Olive Oil** | 1 cup | 1 cup avocado oil | High smoke point, neutral |
| | | 1 cup vegetable/canola oil | More neutral flavor |
| **Coconut Oil** | 1 cup | 1 cup butter | More flavor |
| | | 1 cup vegetable oil | No coconut flavor |

### High-Heat Cooking

| Best For | Smoke Point | Recommended Oils |
|----------|-------------|------------------|
| **Searing/Frying** | 400°F+ | Avocado oil (520°F), refined coconut oil (450°F), canola (400°F) |
| **Sautéing** | 350-400°F | Olive oil (375°F), vegetable oil (400°F) |
| **Baking** | N/A | Any oil, butter, coconut oil |

## Leavening Agent Substitutions

### Baking Powder

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **Baking Powder** | 1 tsp | 1/4 tsp baking soda + 1/2 tsp cream of tartar | Mix fresh each time |
| | | 1/4 tsp baking soda + 1/2 cup buttermilk (replace 1/2 cup liquid) | Adds tang |

### Baking Soda

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **Baking Soda** | 1 tsp | 3 tsp baking powder | May affect taste (slightly bitter) |

**Important:** Baking soda needs acid (buttermilk, lemon juice, vinegar). Baking powder is self-contained.

## Spice & Herb Substitutions

### Fresh vs Dried Herbs

| Fresh Herbs | Amount | Dried Equivalent | Notes |
|-------------|--------|------------------|-------|
| **Fresh Herbs** | 1 tbsp | 1 tsp dried | Dried herbs are more concentrated |
| **Dried Herbs** | 1 tsp | 1 tbsp fresh | Fresh herbs are milder |

### Common Spices

| Original | Amount | Substitute | Notes |
|----------|--------|------------|-------|
| **Cinnamon** | 1 tsp | 1/2 tsp nutmeg | Warmer, spicier |
| **Allspice** | 1 tsp | 1/2 tsp cinnamon + 1/4 tsp cloves + 1/4 tsp nutmeg | DIY allspice |
| **Italian Seasoning** | 1 tbsp | 1 tsp each: basil, oregano, thyme | Mix at home |
| **Pumpkin Pie Spice** | 1 tsp | 1/2 tsp cinnamon + 1/4 tsp ginger + 1/8 tsp nutmeg + 1/8 tsp cloves | Warm spice blend |
| **Garlic Powder** | 1 tsp | 3 fresh garlic cloves (minced) | Fresh is stronger |
| **Onion Powder** | 1 tsp | 1/2 cup fresh onion (minced) | Fresh adds moisture |
